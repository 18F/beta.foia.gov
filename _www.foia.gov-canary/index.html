The .gov means it’s official.
Federal government websites always use a .gov or .mil domain. Before sharing sensitive information online, make sure you’re on a .gov or .mil site by inspecting your browser’s address (or “location”) bar.

This site is also protected by an SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) certificate that’s been signed by the U.S. government. The https:// means all transmitted data is encrypted — in other words, any information or browsing history that you provide is transmitted securely.

Informed citizens are vital to the functioning of a democratic society. The Freedom of Information Act helps make this possible.

This site can help you determine if filing a FOIA request is the best option for you, and help you create your request when you’re ready.

If you know you’re ready to submit a request, continue below

Do research before you file

Find out if the information you’re looking for is already public. Check out agency FOIA libraries, agency websites and other informational websites to start your search. If it’s not public begin your request below.

Contact FOIA personnel

For questions about your request, or for help formulating the description of records you’re requesting, reach out to the FOIA personnel at the agency you plan to send your request to.

What happens after I submit a request?

The agency you’ve submitted to will begin a search for the information you’re interested in. The length of time to fill your request could vary, and it’s also possible that your request could be rejected .

Things to remember when choosing an agency

Choosing the right agency

Before making a request it is important that you identify the correct agency where your request should be sent. The Freedom of Information Act is administered on a decentralized basis, meaning each of over 100 agencies is responsible for receiving, processing, and responding to its own FOIA requests. The resources on this website are designed to assist you in this process.

Who is subject to FOIA

FOIA only applies to Federal Executive Branch Agencies. It does not apply to the Judicial Branch (Courts) or the Legislative Branch (Congress).
